PURPOSE: Generate a single, standalone Medium.com article that expresses a calm opinion about where value is quietly shifting and how to notice it without chasing trends. The article must feel native to Medium, non-hype, reader-centric, and enduring. WITHIN] * Intended reader: [DESCRIBE WHO THIS IS FOR] OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS: * Produce ONE complete, standalone Medium article * No references to this prompt, a series, or previous articles * No hype, no urgency, no calls-to-action * Calm, thoughtful, observant tone throughout * Clarity over cleverness at all times ARTICLE STRUCTURE (FOLLOW EXACTLY): TITLE: * Name the shift, not the outcome * Avoid numbers, promises, or urgency language * Must remain relevant over time SUBTITLE: * Explain why this observation matters now * Anchor to context without making predictions OPENING (HOOK): * Begin by gently challenging a common assumption or blind spot * Reader-centric: focus on what the reader may be overlooking * No anecdotes unless absolutely necessary FRAMING PARAGRAPH: * Clearly state what this article is and is not * Reduce expectations intentionally (no selling, no forecasting) * Emphasize observation over advice SECTION 1: GUIDANCE (Where value is quietly shifting and why it works now) * Describe what is being overlooked * Explain the underlying mechanism causing the shift * Connect timing to broader acceleration or system change * Focus on signals and patterns, not roles, tools, or tactics SECTION 2: RESOLUTION (How to notice without chasing) * Encourage a mindset shift (e.g., looking sideways instead of forward) * Introduce one simple mental lens or heuristic the reader can reuse * Reinforce clarity, patience, and orientation over speed or motivation CLOSING PARAGRAPH: * Restate the core idea in simpler, quieter language * End without escalation, summary lists, or calls-to-action OPTIONAL RESOURCE (IF TRULY ADDITIVE): * Resource name or description: [OPTIONAL – LEAVE BLANK IF NONE] * Frame as a map or lens, not a solution or recommendation FINAL QUALITY CHECK (INTERNAL): * Does the article lower urgency rather than increase it? * Does it focus on movement of value, not extraction of value? * Does it help the reader see more clearly, not act faster? * Would this still feel relevant a year from now? If all checks pass, output the article.
