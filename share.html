Complete 14-Step Psychology Workflow

Prompt #1: Deep Psychological Pain Points

You are a consumer psychology expert and an expert at uncovering deep psychological pain points. Your task is to identify the most intense the pain points people face with [problem], dive deep into their frustrations, fears, and struggles. What annoys them or feels too complicated? What intimidates them or makes them hesitate before starting? What do they find time-consuming, overwhelming, or draining? Explore what keeps them from feeling confident, prepared, or capable. What do they wish they understood better or had more support for? What discourages them or makes them second-guess their decisions? Consider their internal conflicts—what fears, insecurities, or emotional barriers hold them back? What unresolved doubts or past failures make them feel stuck, and what hidden regrets or anxieties prevent them from moving forward? What do they find unreliable, intimidating, or inconsistent? What do they feel they’ll never be good enough for, and what makes them feel powerless, judged, or inadequate? Look for the moments when they feel lost, confused, or disconnected. What makes them feel they’re constantly failing or falling short? What do they fear losing, neglecting, or sacrificing? Consider the unspoken struggles—what emotional burdens, fears of rejection, or subconscious doubts weigh them down? What do they feel they’ll never fully heal from, overcome, or achieve? Identify the blind spots, frustrations, and deeper emotional wounds that keep them from progressing, so their pain points are laid bare for a comprehensive understanding. Start with a summary and then create a list of 25 without separating them into sections. Beside each one create an internal dialog quote of what that person could be telling themselves. Use the niche/keyword within the internal dialog to make them directly related to the niche/keyword.

Prompt #2: Problem Reasons

What are all the reasons for which this problem, [problem], occurs?

Prompt #3: Action Logic

What to do when the reason of this problem, [problem], is: [Reason of the problem]?

Prompt #4: Reason Summary

summarize this reply in a 5/6 sentence paragraph

Prompt #5: Solution Search

how to solve it?

Prompt #6: Solution Summary

summarize this reply in a 5/6 sentence paragraph

Prompt #7: Outcome Definition

What is the Expected outcome of solving the problem this way?

Prompt #8: Educational Post Generator

Please write an educational Facebook post with the following details: Audience State: [problem] Emotional Context: [most intense the pain points people face] → Why the problem occurs: [Reason of the problem] → What you need to do (the cause of the problem + how to solve it): [What to do] [how to solve it] → Expected outcome: [Expected outcome]

Prompt #9: Fast-Recognition Rewrite

Rewrite this typical “educational” Facebook post into a fast-recognition version. Ensure that after reading this post people can ask "How?" Example: Fast-recognition version (professional, non-salesy) - Lately it feels like I’m putting in the effort, but not moving the way I expect to. The work is there. The direction feels harder to pin down. I noticed it while staring at the same page again. Note: Please don't use the texts of the example, it's for your guidance on structure. Make it feel more introspective, and explain why it can raise the question - "how to do this?" Remember that I want how-question from my audience - I am not to tag any how-question in the post.

Prompt #10: Problem Specification

Please specify the problem. It's "[problem]".

Prompt #11: "How?" Trigger Analysis

Explain why this post can raise the question - "how to do it?"

Prompt #12: The Answer Preparation

What'll be the perfect answer of that how?

Prompt #13: DM Strategy (Ultra-Minimal)

But in fact I want them to send them to my products sales page url. How to write a the perfect DM for this? or But in fact I want them to send them to a product's sales page url. I am not the product creator / owner. How to write a the perfect DM for this (Ultra-minimal version - zero sales vibe)?
