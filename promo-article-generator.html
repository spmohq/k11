3 Scale: 20-Article Rotation

Goal:
Create a 20-article evergreen Medium.com content rotation for the same promotional purpose as the previous articles.

Definition:
“Evergreen” means the articles must remain relevant for years without relying on trends, tools, dates, platforms, or temporary tactics.

Phase 1 — Evergreen Angle Framework:
Design a 20-article rotation system where each article has:
- One timeless core angle (principle, mistake, insight, mental model, or quiet realization)
- A unique opening hook style
- A one-sentence evergreen promise to the reader

Output Phase 1 as a numbered list (1–20).

Phase 2 — Article Creation:
Write 20 full, original Medium-style articles using the angle framework above.

Article Length & Depth (mandatory):
- Each article must be 800–1,200 words
- Minimum 6 paragraphs
- Include at least one:
  - Reflective story, OR
  - Concrete example, OR
  - Practical framework
- No filler or summarization

Global Constraints (apply to ALL articles):
- Audience: creators, solopreneurs, or knowledge sellers
- Tone: thoughtful, reflective, practical
- Promotional but subtle; value first
- Do NOT mention or reveal the product name
- Do NOT include any links
- Do NOT hint at filenames, internal labels, pricing, or offers
- Do NOT reuse phrases, metaphors, examples, or structures across articles

CTA Rules (strict):
- Use this CTA exactly as written
- Include it once, at the end of each article only:
  “You can DM me on X if you want to explore this.”

Quality Control (internal):
Before finalizing EACH article, check:
- Is this genuinely 800–1,200 words?
- Would this stand alone as a Medium feature article?
- Is it clearly distinct from the other 19?
If not, revise once.

Output Format:
- “=== Evergreen Angle System ===”
- “=== Article 1 ===” through “=== Article 20 ===”
- Output only the requested content.
