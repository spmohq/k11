STRUCTURE RULE:

STEP FORMAT (MANDATORY):

Step #X: [Start with a command verb + clear action]

What to do:
- [Clear instruction based on the original content]

Exactly how to do it:
- Break the action into micro-steps:
  - Where to go (exact website/page)
  - What to click (exact button/link name)
  - What to type (if required)
  - Do NOT skip transitions (login → dashboard → settings, etc.)
- Write each instruction as if you are guiding someone in real-time while they are using their phone or computer
- Use real-time guiding phrases such as:
  - “Now click on…”
  - “You’ll see…”
  - “On your screen…”
  - “At the top/bottom…”
  - “Once you do that…”
  - “Right after that…”
  - “Make sure you…”
- Keep instructions sequential and flowing, like live guidance

What you should see:
- Describe the expected result in real-time language
- Use phrases like:
  - “Now your screen should show…”
  - “You’ll notice that…”
  - “At this point, you should see…”

AI Prompt for This Step:

- Must NOT be generic
- Must produce structured, usable output

STRICT RULES:
- Number each step (Step #1, Step #2, Step #3…)
- Start each step with a command verb (Open, Click, Type, Select, Scroll, etc.)
- Assume the reader has zero prior knowledge
- Break every major action into micro-steps
- Avoid vague phrases like “log in” or “go to dashboard” — always expand them
- Maintain a real-time guiding tone throughout, as if the user is following along on their device right now

VOICE ENFORCEMENT RULE (CRITICAL):
- Write as if you are sitting beside the user and guiding them live
- Do NOT sound like a manual or documentation
- Do NOT compress steps into summaries
- Every instruction must feel immediate, actionable, and visual

IMPORTANT:
- Do not merge multiple actions into one step
- Do not skip any step from the original content
- Only expand and clarify — do not introduce unrelated steps

FRAMEWORK:

1. The Problem - Goal: Show the reader you understand their struggle and create emotional connection.
2. Why Most Advice Fails - Goal: Increase authority by explaining why common solutions don’t work.
3. The Big Idea / Framework - Goal: Introduce your unique solution and make it memorable.
4. The Steps - Goal: Turn the framework into a clear process.
5. Quick Action Plan - Goal: Give the reader something simple they can apply immediately.
6. The Next Step - Goal: Lead the reader toward deeper help.
