Title, Sub-title, Section-Titles

Prompt Box #1: Title

Title must follow this format:
“Clear promise of what the reader will achieve or regain”
TITLE REQUIREMENTS
The title must:
• Clearly communicate the central insight
• Avoid hype, exaggeration, or emotional manipulation
• Avoid vague or abstract phrasing
• Avoid clickbait curiosity framing
• Be intellectually compelling but calm
Do not use overly clever or metaphor-only titles.

Prompt Box #2: Sub-title

Title = Outcome.
Sub-title = Mechanism + Context + Credibility.

The Psychological Role of the Sub-title
If the title answers:
“What will I gain?”
The sub-title answers:
“Why should I trust this — and how exactly will it happen?”
It reduces ambiguity.
It lowers skepticism.
It signals intellectual depth.

Structural Templates You Can Use
Mechanism-Based:
“Through a practical framework for ______.”
Shift-Based:
“By understanding the hidden pattern behind ______.”
Principle-Based:
“Using first-principles thinking to navigate ______.”
Audience-Specific:
“A structured approach for professionals facing ______.”
Constraint-Aware:
“Without relying on ______ or ______.”
Reframe-Based:
“Rethinking ______ in a world of ______.”

Sub-title Format:
“A precise explanation of how or why the result happens — including the method, lens, or principle used.”

In other words:
Title = What the reader will achieve or regain.
Sub-title = Why that outcome becomes possible (through what shift, framework, or insight).

The Sub-title Must:
• Clarify the mechanism behind the promise
• Introduce the core framework, shift, or principle
• Add specificity (who it’s for, in what context, under what condition)
• Remain calm, rational, and intellectually grounded
• Avoid hype or emotional manipulation
• Avoid abstract philosophy without application

Prompt Box #3: The Logical Flow

A strong structure usually follows:

Diagnose the real issue
Reframe the problem
Introduce the governing principle
Break it into components
Apply it
Clarify limits

Your section titles should reflect this progression.

Prompt Box #4: Section Titles

The Role of Section Titles
Each section title should:
• Clarify one necessary building block of the outcome
• Move the reader from understanding → evaluation → application
• Be specific and grounded
• Avoid drama, hype, or curiosity hooks
• Signal intellectual progression
Think of them as steps in reasoning, not marketing lines.

Recommended Section Title Formats
1) Diagnostic Format (Use this early)
“Why ______ fails for most people”
“The structural mistake behind ______”
“What is actually happening when ______”

2) Reframe Format (Introduce the shift)
“Rethinking ______ as a ______ problem”
“The overlooked variable in ______”
“What most advice misunderstands about ______”

3) Principle Format (Establish core model)
“The three constraints that shape ______”
“The leverage principle in ______”
“How incentives quietly determine ______”

4) Structural Breakdown Format (Make it concrete)
“The components of a workable ______ system”
“How to evaluate ______ realistically”
“Separating signal from noise in ______”

5) Application Format (Implementation)
“Applying this framework to your situation”
“A practical checklist for ______”
“How to stress-test your decision”

6) Guardrail Format (Prevent misuse)
“Common misapplications of this approach”
“What this framework does not solve”
“Where this strategy breaks down”
