Please read and understand the uploaded document.

It's a product I have just bought and yet to read.

“If I just bought this… what would confuse me in the first 10 minutes?”
“What would I hesitate on?”
“Where would I pause and think — what now?”

👉 Think like a confused beginner

Prompt #2: Sourcing the Specific Pain Point

Evaluate the uploaded document as if it were a digital product being submitted to a marketplace.

Your goal is to determine:
- Which specific, real-world problem or crisis of its users it solves
- What are the tangible and business impacts of that problem or crisis
- Extract a clear, concise, and reusable SPECIFIC_PAIN_POINT that can be directly used in messaging frameworks

Required Output Format

[0] Core Placeholder Statement:
- Format strictly as:
  An [ACTOR] who wants [DESIRED OUTCOME] but cannot [SPECIFIC_PAIN_POINT].
- Keep it concise, sharp, and concrete
- Avoid vague phrases such as lack of clarity or struggles with content
- The pain point must be specific, observable, and scenario-based

[1] Problem or Crisis:
- Describe the user's situation BEFORE using this prompt
- Be specific and scenario-based (what exactly are they doing or failing at)
- Avoid generic phrasing
- Focus on practical struggle, not abstract labels

[2] Impacts of Problem or Crisis:
- Describe tangible consequences such as time loss, revenue loss, inconsistency, missed opportunities, burnout
- Include business-level impact where applicable
- Optionally include emotional consequences such as frustration, overwhelm, self-doubt

[3] Extracted SPECIFIC_PAIN_POINT (Reusable):
- Write ONLY the pain point phrase
- Must be directly usable inside:
  but cannot [THIS]
- Keep it short, concrete, and high-impact
- Example styles:
  consistently come up with scroll-stopping hooks
  turn ideas into engaging posts that drive DMs
