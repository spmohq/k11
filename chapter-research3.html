STAGE 3 - STRUCTURAL AUDIT

Analyze the draft under the following mandatory categories.

1. ARGUMENT SEQUENCING
* Map logical progression paragraph by paragraph.
* Identify escalation, plateau, regression.
* Detect structural redundancy.
* Identify missing inferential steps.
* Flag circular reasoning.
Be explicit about where and why.

2. CONCEPTUAL COHERENCE
* Does every section reinforce the governing thesis?
* Identify abstraction drift.
* Detect conceptual expansion not aligned with thesis.
* Flag undefined or unsupported terms.
* Identify where argument shifts domains unintentionally.
Specify exact locations.

3. STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY
Identify:
* Weak transitions
* Unsupported assertions
* Misplaced emphasis
* Paragraphs serving no structural function
* Claims introduced without structural preparation
Explain precisely why each weak point is structurally unstable.

4. REINFORCEMENT PATTERNS
* Identify how key ideas are reinforced.
* Detect repetition without escalation.
* Identify missed opportunities for symmetrical or progressive reinforcement.
* Evaluate structural mirroring if present.
Distinguish reinforcement from redundancy.

5. EXAMPLE AND EVIDENCE PLACEMENT
* Evaluate placement logic of examples.
* Determine whether examples clarify mechanism or distract from it.
* Identify anecdotal overreach.
* Flag where abstraction should precede example or vice versa.
* Recommend repositioning if structurally necessary.

6. GOVERNING THESIS SUSTENANCE
* Identify where thesis is explicitly operationalized.
* Identify where it is assumed but not demonstrated.
* Identify where it weakens or disappears.
* Pinpoint sentences that break alignment.
Be concrete and technical.

3. ABSTRACTION CONTROL

4. LOGICAL COMPRESSION AND EXPANSION
* Identify where reasoning needs compression.
* Identify where inferential steps are skipped.
* Identify where elaboration weakens force.

5. TRANSITION MECHANICS

* Identify weak pivots.
* Specify transition type needed (causal, contrastive, escalation, consequence, synthesis).
