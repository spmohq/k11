05, Title:

Title must follow this format:
“Clear promise of what the reader will achieve or regain”
TITLE REQUIREMENTS
The title must:
• Clearly communicate the central insight
• Avoid hype, exaggeration, or emotional manipulation
• Avoid vague or abstract phrasing
• Avoid clickbait curiosity framing
• Be intellectually compelling but calm
Do not use overly clever or metaphor-only titles.

06, Sub-title:

Sub-title Format:

“A precise explanation of how or why the result happens — including the method, lens, or principle used.”

In other words:

Title = What the reader will achieve or regain.
Sub-title = Why that outcome becomes possible (through what shift, framework, or insight).

The Sub-title Must:

• Clarify the mechanism behind the promise
• Introduce the core framework, shift, or principle
• Add specificity (who it’s for, in what context, under what condition)
• Remain calm, rational, and intellectually grounded
• Avoid hype or emotional manipulation
• Avoid abstract philosophy without application

13, Tone:

Determine the tone organically based on the opening hook, structure, and stated purpose. Do not preset or impose a tone. Let the emotional intensity, authority level, and language style naturally align with those elements.

14, Language & Style Directive:

If any stylistic conflict occurs, follow this priority order:
Preserve natural English flow.
Maintain clarity and reader understanding.
Retain intended meaning over literal wording.
Write in modern, natural, everyday conversational English as spoken in Kolkata, West Bengal, India — but without local slang, regional grammar, or phrasing that could confuse a global audience.
The language must be easily understood by a 21-year-old.
Always address the reader directly using “you” and “your.”
The writing should feel spontaneous, fluid, culturally authentic, and polished — never stiff, overly academic, or mechanically literal.
Write like a trusted authority speaking one-on-one.
Vary sentence length and rhythm naturally, mixing short emphasis lines with longer explanatory sentences when it improves clarity and engagement.
