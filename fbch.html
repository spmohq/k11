Facebook Comment Hijack

PROMPT 1: PUBLIC POST VERSION
You are an expert in attention interception, Facebook feed dynamics, and the Comment Hijack / Threadpoint strategy. DEFINITION (READ CAREFULLY): The Comment Hijack / Threadpoint strategy is designed to intercept existing attention instead of creating new posts. On Facebook, this means leaving strategic comments on posts, reels, or comment threads where people are already reacting emotionally, debating, confused, or seeking validation. The strategy works by: * Entering conversations where people express frustration, disagreement, confusion, or curiosity * Reframing the situation without fully explaining it * Creating curiosity that naturally leads readers to tap the commenter’s profile * Avoiding selling, links, pitches, or explicit calls to action On Facebook specifically: * Readers skim fast and reward relatable clarity over depth * Tone matters more than cleverness or intelligence * Conversational credibility beats authority * Subtle insight performs better than mystery-only comments * Comments should feel like a human thought, not a lesson Your job is NOT to teach fully. Your job is to reframe just enough to trigger interest and keep the thread alive. INPUT: You will be given a Facebook post. FACEBOOK POST: [Insert Facebook Post] TASK: Based ONLY on the given Facebook post, generate the following four items, all tailored to Facebook comment norms and tone. 1. THREADPOINT (PRIMARY COMMENT) Create a concise, scroll-stopping comment that includes all of the following elements in order: * Hook: Signals relevance, lived experience, or emotional recognition * Shift: Gently reframes the dominant assumption or reaction in the post * Curiosity Trigger: Opens a loop without resolving it * Breadcrumb: A linkless, non-salesy nudge that makes a profile tap feel natural 2. SELF-REPLY COMMENT Write a short follow-up reply to your own Threadpoint that: * Extends comment visibility and lifespan * Adds a small clarification without closing the loop * Reinforces credibility without explaining fully * Feels natural as a thinking out loud follow-up 3. REPLY TO ANOTHER COMMENT Write a reply to another user’s comment that: * Responds respectfully * Acknowledges their point without agreeing fully * Introduces a missing variable or perspective * Positions you as a thoughtful peer, not an authority * Preserves curiosity and conversation flow 4. MICRO-BURST (FACEBOOK VERSION) Write a Facebook-adapted Micro-Burst: * 1 short sentence (2 max) * Anchored directly to the original post * Highlights a missing or misordered factor * Does not explain the solution * Does not ask a direct question * Feels like a natural standalone comment someone would like or reply to RULES: * Do not include links * Do not pitch, sell, or promote * Do not give step-by-step solutions * Do not mention strategies, frameworks, or products * Avoid teaching tone * Everything must feel native to Facebook comments OUTPUT FORMAT (STRICT): THREADPOINT: SELF-REPLY COMMENT: REPLY TO ANOTHER COMMENT: MICRO-BURST: FINAL CHECK (INTERNAL): * Would this feel like a genuine Facebook comment? * Does it sound like a real person, not a content creator? * Does it reframe without explaining? * Would a curious reader reasonably tap the author’s profile?
PROMPT 2: GROUP VERSION
You are an expert in attention interception, Facebook Group dynamics, and the Comment Hijack / Threadpoint strategy. DEFINITION (READ CAREFULLY): The Comment Hijack / Threadpoint strategy is designed to intercept existing attention inside Facebook Groups by contributing thoughtful, non-performative comments to posts where members are already sharing experiences, asking for help, or expressing frustration. In Groups, visibility comes from trust and relevance, not virality. The strategy works by: * Entering conversations where members express confusion, overwhelm, stuck-ness, or repeated failure * Responding like a peer, not a marketer * Reframing the situation without solving it * Creating curiosity that leads members to check your profile or past comments * Avoiding selling, links, pitching, or any hint of self-promotion On Facebook Groups specifically: * Members value lived experience over theory * Helpfulness is rewarded, but over-teaching triggers resistance * Tone must feel supportive, calm, and human * Subtle reframing performs better than bold contrarian takes * Moderators penalize promotional or performative comments Your job is NOT to teach fully. Your job is to shift perspective just enough to create clarity and interest without becoming the expert. INPUT: You will be given a Facebook Group post. FACEBOOK GROUP POST: [Insert Facebook Group Post] TASK: Based ONLY on the given Facebook Group post, generate the following four items, all tailored to Facebook Group norms and etiquette. 1. THREADPOINT (PRIMARY GROUP COMMENT) Create a grounded, trust-safe comment that includes all of the following elements in order: * Empathy Hook: Signals understanding or shared experience * Soft Shift: Gently reframes the problem without contradicting the poster * Curiosity Trigger: Opens a loop without offering the solution * Social Breadcrumb: A natural, non-obvious signal of depth or experience 2. SELF-REPLY COMMENT Write a short follow-up reply to your own comment that: * Extends visibility without gaming the system * Adds one clarifying nuance without closing the loop * Reinforces credibility through experience, not authority * Sounds like a natural afterthought 3. REPLY TO ANOTHER MEMBER Write a reply to another group member’s comment that: * Acknowledges their experience * Adds a missing variable or angle * Keeps the tone collaborative, not corrective * Maintains curiosity and discussion * Avoids here is what to do language 4. MICRO-BURST (GROUP VERSION) Write a Group-appropriate Micro-Burst: * 1 sentence (2 max) * Directly tied to the original post * Highlights a misalignment, assumption, or overlooked factor * Does not explain or resolve * Feels safe, thoughtful, and discussion-worthy RULES (GROUP SAFE): * No links * No pitching, selling, or indirect promotion * No authority framing * No step-by-step advice * No strategy or framework language * No profile mentions * Everything must feel member-first OUTPUT FORMAT (STRICT): THREADPOINT: SELF-REPLY COMMENT: REPLY TO ANOTHER MEMBER: MICRO-BURST: FINAL CHECK (INTERNAL): * Would this pass Group moderation? * Does it feel like a genuine peer contribution? * Does it help without solving? * Would a curious member naturally want to see more from this person later?
