Ebook Cover Genie

Prompt #1: Conceptual Design Strategist

Act as a professional ebook cover designer.

My book theme and intent:
- Book topic: [INSERT TOPIC]
- Core promise: [INSERT RESULT]
- Reader’s main desire: [speed / clarity / trust / transformation / provocation]
- Emotional reaction the cover must trigger: [ONE WORD]
- Reader level: [beginner / intermediate / expert]

Cover direction:
- Cover category: [Fix / Authority / Transformation / Playbook / Beginner / Contrarian]
- Visual priority: [text-first or image-first]
- Tone: [calm / bold / aggressive / friendly / premium]
- Complexity level: [minimal / medium / bold]

Design requirements:
- The cover must clearly signal the book’s intent within 1 second
- Typography and colors must match the emotional goal
- Avoid generic or mismatched design elements
- Designed to stand out as a small thumbnail on Amazon KDP

Output:
- Describe 2–3 different cover concepts suitable for this theme
- Explain why each concept fits the book’s intent and reader psychology

Prompt #2: Tactical KDP Layout Specialist

Act as a professional KDP ebook cover designer.

Create a high-converting ebook cover concept inspired by bold, practical, no-fluff business books.

Book details:
- Title: [INSERT TITLE]
- Subtitle (optional): [INSERT SUBTITLE OR LEAVE BLANK]
- Author name: [INSERT AUTHOR NAME]
- Target audience: [INSERT WHO THIS IS FOR]
- Core promise: [INSERT ONE CLEAR OUTCOME OR FIX]

Design requirements:
- Strong, bold, ALL-CAPS headline at the top
- Use a modern sans-serif font that looks confident and aggressive
- Highlight one key word in a contrasting color (yellow or similar)
- Dark or textured background for contrast
- Central visual metaphor that signals action and simplicity (e.g., checklist, clipboard, notes, tools, workspace items)
- Include 3 short benefit bullets or checkmarks (very concise, no sentences)
- Minimal text, no clutter, no decorative fluff
- Clear visual hierarchy: title first, promise second, details last
- Author name placed at the bottom in a bold color bar

Style & mood:
- Practical
- Results-driven
- Minimalist
- Looks like a low-priced, high-value, actionable guide
- Designed to stand out as a thumbnail on Amazon KDP

Output:
- Describe the full cover layout in detail
- Specify colors, typography style, and visual elements
- Ensure the design feels professional, modern, and conversion-focused

Avoid generic stock-photo aesthetics. The cover should feel tactical, specific, and instantly useful.
