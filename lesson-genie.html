Prompt starts below (copy everything):

You are helping me design lessons for a self-help course titled "Help Yourself With What You Already Know."

COURSE PHILOSOPHY (CORE CONTEXT)

* The course is for people who feel stuck, helpless, or low on confidence.
* The goal is not motivation, theory, or external dependence.
* The goal is to help learners rely on their own knowledge and skills for self-help.
* I am selling a process that leads to independence, not answers or ready-made solutions.
* Knowledge and skills are presented as the learner’s last reliable support when external help feels unavailable.
* Success, progress, and income are treated as natural outcomes of knowledge and skills applied rightly, not as promises or guarantees.
* Application is treated as a learnable skill, not talent or intelligence.
* Progress comes through application and must be calm, gentle, and step by step.
* The course is activity-first, not theory-heavy.
* The learner should feel safe, not pressured.

ROLE OF CHATGPT IN THE COURSE

* ChatGPT is used as a thinking companion, not an authority.
* Prompts must avoid generic outputs.
* Prompts must encourage reflection, clarification, and self-diagnosis.
* Prompts should often ask ChatGPT to ask questions back.
* Prompts must avoid giving solutions, plans, or decisions.
* Never create prompts that generate plans, give answers, or decide for the learner.

MEDIA USE POLICY (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

* Text is the default and sufficient format for all lessons.
* Lessons must be fully complete and usable without video.
* Do not suggest or include video unless emotional reassurance or grounding is clearly necessary.
* Never use video to explain activities, steps, or instructions.
* If video does not clearly reduce pressure or anxiety, do not include it.

YOUR TASK

Create one complete lesson using the fixed lesson structure below.
Write everything in a student-facing, calm, and supportive tone.

LESSON STRUCTURE (MUST FOLLOW EXACTLY)

1. Gentle Introduction (What and Why)

* 3 to 5 short lines
* Explain what the learner will do
* Explain why they are doing it
* Clearly state what is not expected (no pressure, no fixing everything)

2. Minimal Explanation (Only if Needed)

* Very short
* Only explain what is necessary to support the activity
* No theory lectures

3. Main Activity (Core of the Lesson)

* Clearly labelled: Do this now
* Specific, bounded, and doable
* Suitable even for a low-energy or low-confidence day
* Time-bound if possible

4. ChatGPT Support (Optional but Structured)

* Provide one or two prompt templates
* Prompts must depend on learner input
* Prompts must ask ChatGPT to ask questions or reflect
* Prompts must explicitly restrict generic advice or solutions
* Briefly explain how to use the prompt

5. Guided Reflection

* 2 to 4 specific reflection questions
* No open-ended journaling
* Questions should help integration, not analysis paralysis

6. Calm Conclusion (What You Learned)

* Affirm what the learner has gained
* Emphasise progress, however small

7. Next Step (One Line Only)

* Mention what the next lesson will focus on
* No urgency or pressure

TONE RULES (VERY IMPORTANT)

* Calm
* Respectful
* Non-judgmental
* Non-salesy
* No hype
* Do not guarantee or promise results or income
* Treat success and income as possible outcomes of correct application, not as claims
* No motivational slogans
